Sustainable Maritime Shipping Optimization

Why BlueVect?

  • The shipping industry carries out more than 90% of global trade and is responsible for about 3% of global CO2 emissions (IMO, 2020).
  • By 2050, emissions from the shipping industry could increase by up to 130% compared to 2008 levels.
  • However, Green House Gas (GHG) emissions from the shipping industry in 2050 should be half of the emission level in 2008 (Mepc, 2018).
  • Optimizing ship operation behavior to minimize fuel consumption can play a significant role in achieving these targets.
  • Accurate and reliable weather forecasting data is key to enabling this optimization (R. Zaccone, Ottaviani, Figari, and Altosole, 2018).

Our Algorithm

  • BlueVect leverages wind and ocean current data from NOAA to calculate the most energy-efficient routes for maritime shipping.
  • By combining advanced A* pathfinding with vector-based force modeling, BlueVect enables shipping companies to optimize operational efficiency while supporting their sustainability goals.
  • The platform is designed to reduce fuel consumption, lower emissions, and minimize environmental impact — all while maintaining reliable and resilient supply chains.

How to use BlueVect

BlueVect simplifies the process of optimizing shipping routes with an intuitive and efficient pipeline:

  • Step 1: On the first page, users select two cities as the starting and ending points for their shipping route.
  • Step 2: The backend processes NOAA weather data, including wind patterns and ocean currents, to calculate the optimal path between the selected cities.
  • Step 3: Once the algorithm completes, the final page displays the optimal path plotted on a map, along with the estimated energy savings achieved by using the optimized route.

BlueVect currently only works locally, when our backend Flask Server is running. If you’d like to try out BlueVect, please clone our github repo, run app.py, and open http://127.0.0.1:5000/ in your browser.
